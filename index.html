What we check — and what we can't confirm yet

Japan's official visa-photo pages are not consistently published with pixel-level specs, so we're upfront about what's a common convention versus what we've directly verified. Always confirm size and requirements with the specific consulate or visa application center you're using.

Some consulates cite 45×45mm, others 35×45mm, and some accept the US-style 2×2in. We default to 45×45mm as the most commonly cited size, but this is not universally confirmed — please check your consulate's page before printing.

  • Print size — defaults to 45×45mm (common consulate convention) — confirm with your consulate, as some cite 35×45mm.
  • Head height — targets roughly 32–36mm (crown to chin), an ICAO-style convention cited by several sources but not independently verified.
  • Plain white background — after automatic cutout, no shadows or patterns.
  • Recency & expression — the checklist reminds you the photo should be recent (within 6 months), neutral expression, no hat.
  • No face reshaping — segmentation and cropping only; we don't slim or resize facial geometry.
  • Privacy — segmentation and matting run on your iPhone; no separate upload to our servers for processing.

This app does not claim ICAO compliance or a confirmed file-size limit for Japan — no primary source could be independently verified at the time of writing. Final acceptance is always at the discretion of the consulate or visa application center you use.
